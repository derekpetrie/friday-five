AI in Marketing
5 Bullet Friday
May 15, 2026

This week's pattern: the gap between what AI can do and what your team is set up to absorb. Three major studies landed the same verdict — readiness beats spending.

1
Google Rewires Search Bidding for AI
  • New "journey-aware" bidding (in beta) lets Google learn from your full lead-to-sale path, not just the first click.
  • Smart Bidding Exploration expands to Shopping and Performance Max; demand-led pacing auto-shifts spend when buyer intent spikes.
  • For B2B lead gen on Google, this is the first bidding tool built for long sales cycles — get into the beta.
Google Ads & Commerce Blog
2
Cutting Staff for AI Doesn't Improve ROI
  • Gartner surveyed 350 execs: 80% of companies piloting autonomous AI cut staff, but those cuts don't correlate with better returns.
  • The winners invested in new roles and operating models — not fewer people.
  • Next time someone suggests swapping three FTEs for an AI tool, this is the data you bring to the conversation.
Gartner
3
CMOs Spend 15% on AI They Can't Scale
  • Gartner's 2026 CMO Spend Survey: 15.3% of marketing budgets go to AI, but only 30% of teams can actually scale it.
  • Budgets are flat at 7.8% of revenue — every AI dollar comes from somewhere else.
  • AI-ready teams spend 21.3% and get above-average total budgets — readiness unlocks more investment, not less.
Gartner
4
Most Winning Marketing Tests Fail at Scale
  • GrowthLoop surveyed 300+ marketers: 77% say "winning" experiments fail when they try to scale them.
  • The bottleneck isn't AI — it's fragmented data. Only 46% have a single source of truth for customer data.
  • Before you add more AI-powered experiments, fix the pipeline that turns a winning test into a live campaign.
GrowthLoop via PR Newswire
5
HBR's Blueprint for the AI Marketing Org
  • HBR introduces the "brand code" — a machine-readable layer encoding your strategy, voice, and rules so AI agents can act on them.
  • The model: AI agents handle execution at scale; your team shifts to direction, judgment, and quality control.
  • Start your AI reorg with what the AI needs to know about your brand — not which tools to buy.
Harvard Business Review
