This week's through-line: everyone's buying the AI future, almost nobody's operationalized it, and your audience can tell.

Salesforce Buys Its Way to Agent Empire
  • Salesforce acquired Fin (formerly Intercom) for $3.6B — its third deal this month, after M3ter and Contentful.
  • Fin's AI agent resolves 76% of support tickets without humans across chat, email, WhatsApp, and phone.
  • If you're on Salesforce, your platform is being rebuilt around you — plan for an agent-first stack, not a bolt-on.

2
96% of CMOs Talk AI; 8% Do It
  • BCG surveyed 300 CMOs: nearly all acknowledge AI transformation, but only 8% run autonomous multi-agent campaigns.
  • 43% now spend $15M+ on marketing AI, up from 28% last year, but most lack the data infrastructure agents need.
  • The bottleneck isn't budget or buy-in — it's data pipelines, talent, and the will to actually rewire operations.
BCG
3
AI Joins Your Buyer's Committee
  • HBR shows AI intermediaries are rewriting B2B vendor discovery — outspending competitors no longer guarantees you get surfaced.
  • In one case, a smaller pharma brand outranked a bigger rival because its data was structured for how AI finds answers.
  • Your content now serves two audiences: the human who decides and the AI agent that filters what they see.
Harvard Business Review
4
Half Your Audience Thinks Content Got Worse
  • Gartner surveyed consumers: 49% say GenAI has degraded content quality, rising to 57% among Gen Z and millennials.
  • Volume is up, trust is down — AI content increases what people encounter but not what they value.
  • The brands that win next year invest in recognizable, credibly human content — not just more of it.
Demand Gen Report
5
$65M Bet on Marketing's Agent Layer
  • Gradial raised $65M Series C at a $675M valuation for AI agents that execute across existing enterprise martech stacks.
  • It automates content ops, brand compliance, and multi-variant assembly inside your current CMS — augmenting, not replacing.
  • Money is flowing to the orchestration layer, not more point tools — a signal for where your next platform dollar goes.
