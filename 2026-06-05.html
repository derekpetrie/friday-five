AI in Marketing
5 Bullet Friday
June 5, 2026

AI shipped more autonomy this week. The harder question — who trusts it, and with what — is where the real story lives.

1
ChatGPT Ads Go Full Performance
  • OpenAI activated cost-per-action ads in ChatGPT, joining CPC and CPM to complete the performance bidding stack.
  • It's a pilot now, but OpenAI projects $102B in ad revenue by 2030 — expect aggressive scaling.
  • Allocate a small test budget before competition arrives; high-intent ad channels like this are rare.
Digiday
2
Salesforce Ships Four Marketing Agents
  • Four new agents launched at Connections — Piper and Hunter run prospecting, Content Agent builds campaigns from a brief.
  • The pitch is humans own strategy, agents own execution — but real adoption still lags the vision.
  • If you're on Salesforce, these are live now; the real blocker is governance, not technology.
Salesforce Ben
3
Coca-Cola: 14 Days to One Hour
  • Coca-Cola built "Fuel Light 360," an AI scenario tool that replaced two weeks of budget analysis with one meeting.
  • Teams stopped arguing about whose data was right and started making allocation choices in real time.
  • If your budget reviews still need follow-up analyses and reconciliation cycles, that's the process to redesign first.
CFO Dive
4
UK Splits AI Search from Organic
  • The CMA ordered Google to let publishers opt out of AI Overviews without losing organic rankings — a global first.
  • Google has nine months to comply; if major publishers pull out, remaining sources gain outsized AI citation share.
  • Expect the EU and US to follow — content licensing and AI indexing just became a separate strategy from SEO.
GOV.UK
5
Buyers Want AI Help, Not Authority
  • Gartner: 31% of consumers will let AI narrow choices, but only 11% will let it make the purchase.
  • 62% called GenAI shopping info a waste of time; 54% double-checked everything it told them.
  • If you're embedding AI in the buyer journey, keep humans in the decision seat — the trust gap is real.
