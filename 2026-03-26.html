AI in Marketing
5 Bullet Friday
March 26, 2026

Agencies are dismantling the holdco model to fund AI platforms, ChatGPT is selling ads with broken measurement, and a new study confirms half of us are already marketing to machines instead of buyers. This week wasn't incremental.

1
Agency Holdcos Are Betting Everything on AI

WPP dismantled its 40-year holding company structure this week, consolidating Ogilvy, VML, and AKQA under a single brand and redirecting $676M in savings into WPP Open — its agentic marketing platform. Omnicom simultaneously doubled its IPG merger savings target to $1.5B, with $1B coming from labor cuts, while enrolling 30+ brands in the ChatGPT ad pilot on its Omni AI platform. The pattern isn't restructuring for efficiency — both companies are making the same bet that proprietary AI infrastructure beats talent as the durable differentiator. If you're heading into an agency review this year, the most important question on the brief is no longer "who's on our account" but "what does your AI platform know about our category."

Campaign Asia
2
ChatGPT Ads Are Live. Measurement Isn't.

Omnicom, WPP, and Dentsu have enrolled brand clients in OpenAI's advertising pilot, and the early numbers are mixed: click-through rates at 0.91% — seven times below Google Search — a technical bug blocking access to campaign analytics, but impressions up 600% in March alone. OpenAI hired a Meta advertising veteran to lead ad sales, which signals this is a serious channel build, not a sandbox. Treat this the way savvy B2B teams treated LinkedIn ads in 2012: high-intent signal, unproven format, and a compounding advantage for brands that learn the mechanics before measurement is clean. The $200K minimum makes it enterprise-only today, but watch how fast that floor drops.

Adweek
3
51% of Marketers Now Target Machines, Not Buyers

A study released today by Toluna, AMA–New York, and Charney Research found that 51% of marketers are already running campaigns targeting AI models and web crawlers — not human buyers — while 82% expect consumers to benefit from AI versus only 42% of consumers who agree. In B2B the trust gap is narrower, but the audience-bifurcation problem is real: your content now needs to perform for both human decision-makers and the AI agents those buyers use to pre-screen vendors. That's two audiences, two briefs, and two sets of success metrics. Most of our teams are running one program and hoping it crosses over.

GlobeNewswire
4
AI Outbound Is Collapsing Under Its Own Weight

DemandView CEO Chris Rack put a number on what many of us have felt: 95% of all B2B outbound messages will receive zero engagement. AI-driven volume has created a tragedy of the commons — every team's output quality degrades as every team's output quantity scales, and buyers have learned to filter everything. The practical response isn't "use less AI" — it's that differentiation has moved up the stack, into the strategic and creative judgment AI can't synthesize from public information. If your ABM sequences are producing more touches than you can personally vouch for, you're likely contributing to the exact pipeline problem you're being asked to solve.

Demand Gen Report
5
Your Brand Must Become Machine-Readable

A March–April HBR piece from researchers at King's College London and Emory's Goizueta Business School argues that AI agents — not human buyers — are now doing the first-pass of vendor evaluation, filtering and ranking options before a person touches a keyboard. Brands that haven't structured their data, certifications, and content for AI-agent discoverability won't make the consideration set regardless of how strong their human-facing marketing is. This isn't a future-state problem: enterprise B2B buying already involves AI-assisted research, and most of us have no process for monitoring what Perplexity, Claude, or ChatGPT says about our product category. The question for Monday: do you know what your buyer's AI says about you, and do you have anyone responsible for changing it?
