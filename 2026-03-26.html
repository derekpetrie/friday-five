AI in Marketing
5 Bullet Friday
March 26, 2026

Agencies are dismantling the holdco model to fund AI platforms, ChatGPT is selling ads with broken measurement, and a new study confirms half of us are already marketing to machines instead of buyers. This week wasn't incremental.

1
Agency Holdcos Are Betting Everything on AI
  • WPP collapsed its legacy structure and redirected $676M into its agentic platform; Omnicom doubled merger savings to $1.5B, mostly from labor cuts.
  • Both companies are betting proprietary AI infrastructure, not talent, is the durable differentiator.
  • In your next agency review, ask what their AI platform knows about your category — that's the new table stakes.
Campaign Asia
2
ChatGPT Ads Are Live. Measurement Isn't.
  • Omnicom, WPP, and Dentsu are in OpenAI's ad pilot; CTRs lag Google Search 7x, but impressions surged 600% in March.
  • OpenAI hired a Meta ad-sales veteran and set a $200K minimum — this is a serious channel build, not a sandbox.
  • Treat it like LinkedIn ads in 2012: learn the mechanics now and compound the advantage before measurement matures.
Adweek
3
51% of Marketers Now Target Machines, Not Buyers
  • A Toluna/AMA study found 51% of marketers already target AI models and crawlers, while a 40-point trust gap separates marketer optimism from consumer reality.
  • Your content now serves two audiences: human decision-makers and the AI agents those buyers use to pre-screen vendors.
  • That means two briefs and two success metrics — most teams are running one program and hoping it crosses over.
GlobeNewswire
4
AI Outbound Is Collapsing Under Its Own Weight
  • 95% of B2B outbound now gets zero engagement — AI-driven volume created a tragedy of the commons.
  • Differentiation has moved up the stack into strategic and creative judgment AI can't synthesize from public data.
  • If your ABM sequences produce more touches than you can personally vouch for, you're fueling the problem you're solving.
Demand Gen Report
5
Your Brand Must Become Machine-Readable
  • HBR research shows AI agents now do the first pass of vendor evaluation, filtering options before any human touches a keyboard.
  • Brands without structured data and AI-optimized content won't make the consideration set, regardless of human-facing marketing.
  • Find out what your buyer's AI says about you today — and assign someone to own that narrative.
Harvard Business Review
