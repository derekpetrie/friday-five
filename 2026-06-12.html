AI in Marketing
5 Bullet Friday
June 12, 2026

AI adoption hit a wall this week — not of technology, but of trust, measurement, and accountability.

1
Two-Thirds of Google Searches Go Nowhere
  • SparkToro's 2026 study: 68% of U.S. Google searches now end without a click, up from 60% two years ago.
  • AI Overviews cut click-through by roughly 60% when they appear — and they show on one in five searches.
  • If your pipeline starts with organic traffic, the math changed; start measuring citation share, not just clicks.
SparkToro
2
Nearly Every CMO Uses AI — Few See Returns
  • Gartner symposium data: 98% of CMOs now use or pilot AI, but fewer than a third report meaningful results.
  • The diagnosis is a "competency trap" — quick task-level wins feel like progress and prevent the harder strategic work.
  • Ask whether your AI investments are making you faster at the same work or changing what work you do.
Gartner via Let's Data Science
3
Ad Platforms Race to Plug Into AI Agents
  • AdRoll, Lifesight, Measured, and Stensul all shipped MCP connectors this week, letting AI tools query campaign data in plain English.
  • The pattern: your ad platforms are becoming backends for AI assistants, not just dashboards you log into.
  • When evaluating martech renewals, ask "does it support MCP?" — that's fast becoming the interoperability litmus test.
MarTech
4
Half of Consumers Say AI Worsened Content
  • Gartner: 49% of U.S. consumers say GenAI has made content quality worse; 57% of Gen Z and Millennials agree.
  • More volume, less value — and your youngest buyers are the most skeptical, not the most forgiving.
  • The moat is fewer, better, human-edited pieces — not more AI-generated volume.
Gartner via Demand Gen Report
5
New York Now Requires AI Disclosure in Ads
  • As of June 10, any ad shown in New York using AI-generated "synthetic performers" must carry a conspicuous disclosure.
  • Fines start at $1K per violation and $5K for repeats — applies regardless of where you're based.
  • Build your disclosure workflow now; California and Illinois have similar bills moving.
