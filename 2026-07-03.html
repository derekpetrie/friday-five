AI in Marketing
5 Bullet Friday
July 3, 2026

Everyone’s using AI. Almost nobody’s reorganized around it. This week, the gap showed up in the data.

1
OpenAI Is Now an Ad Platform
  • At Cannes, OpenAI projected $2.5B in 2026 ad revenue from ChatGPT’s approaching-1B weekly users.
  • Over 2,000 brands already run “chat card” ads through Criteo at $25–$60 CPMs.
  • Your media plan now has a third major gatekeeper — start testing before CPMs climb.
Digital Applied
2
Martech Budgets Hit a Five-Year Low
  • Gartner’s 2026 CMO survey: martech fell to 19.4% of budgets while paid media hit 31.4% — both five-year extremes.
  • Only 9% of CMOs rate their orgs as fully AI-optimized; brand awareness achievement dropped from 57% to 49%.
  • We’re buying more AI tools and hitting fewer targets — the gap is operational, not technological.
Chief Marketer
3
AI Trust Fell 28 Points in One Year
  • Fractl’s Q2 survey: 70% of consumers use AI search more, but perceived helpfulness dropped from 82% to 54%.
  • Consumers who say heavy AI use lowers brand trust doubled year-over-year, from 20% to 40%.
  • Think twice before branding yourself “AI-powered” — customers may see it as a warning label, not a feature.
ContentGrip
4
EU AI Act Hits in 30 Days
  • Article 50 takes effect August 2: mandatory disclosure of AI-generated images, audio, video, and public-interest text.
  • Human editorial review exempts some content — a critical detail for content marketing teams.
  • If you run AI-generated creative in EU campaigns, your compliance review starts now, not next quarter.
Sidley Austin
5
60% Use AI. 10% Redesigned for It.
  • McKinsey at Cannes: nearly 60% of marketers use AI weekly, but only 10% have redesigned workflows for real impact.
  • “Mediocrity at scale” is the risk when every team uses the same models with the same prompts.
  • The edge isn’t adopting AI — it’s whether you’ve actually changed how your team works.
