AI in Marketing
5 Bullet Friday
April 10, 2026

Five stories, one thread this week: power is shifting inside the AI marketing stack — in pricing, in M&A, in your own job description, and in what “real” even means for a brand.

1
HubSpot will only charge when AI works
  • Breeze agents move to outcome pricing April 14: $0.50 per resolved chat, $1 per recommended lead.
  • First major martech vendor to bet on results instead of seats or tokens.
  • Make this the new baseline in every 2026 renewal — if your vendor still charges for access, ask why.
HubSpot
2
Canva just bought its way into martech
  • Canva acquired Simtheory (AI agents) and Ortto (marketing automation, 11,000 customers in 190 countries).
  • A design tool is now competing with HubSpot, Marketo, and Adobe Express in one bundle.
  • Expect a consolidation conversation at your next renewal — two or three line items could collapse into one.
TechCrunch
3
Forrester: your job just got bigger
  • Forrester says AI doesn’t shrink the CMO role — it expands it into growth orchestrator and brand governor.
  • “Brand steward” now includes how answer engines talk about you when no human is looking.
  • Use it in your next board deck — the AI reorg isn’t cost cutting, it’s pulling control back to your team.
Forrester
4
Meta is quietly running your ads
  • Advantage+, Andromeda, and Manus are switched on by default — one brand’s 300-asset brief ballooned to 1,000.
  • Big brands are still refusing to hand creative to the black box, and legal teams are backing them.
  • Add a monthly platform audit — what Meta turned on last week is your media strategy now.
Marketing Brew
5
‘No AI’ is becoming a brand claim
  • Aerie, Le Creuset, and Dove are running “human-made” as a selling point; half of consumers say they prefer it.
  • Aerie’s Pamela Anderson spot became its most-liked Instagram post ever and Q4 sales rose 23%.
  • Your B2B version is “human-written” thought leadership — provenance may matter before every blog sounds the same.
