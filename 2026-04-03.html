AI in Marketing
5 Bullet Friday
April 3, 2026

AI is compressing the middle layer of marketing — the agencies, the point solutions, the organic rankings that used to come free. What replaces them costs money or requires restructuring. Plan accordingly.

1
Your Brand Shows Up in 3% of AI Answers
  • Walker Sands analyzed 45M keywords across 828 B2B companies — AI Overviews appear in half of searches, but the typical brand gets cited in just 3%.
  • Traditional search rankings don't predict whether AI will mention you — topic authority and structured content matter more.
  • If you're still measuring SEO success by page-one rankings alone, you're missing where your buyers are actually reading.
GlobeNewsWire / Walker Sands
2
Forrester Named the B2B Orgs Getting AI Right
  • IBM moved to audience-centric GTM, Autodesk built a centralized AI content engine tied to pipeline, and Verizon Business replaced NPS surveys with a predictive CX index.
  • The common thread: each tied AI to one specific business metric instead of sprinkling it across existing workflows.
  • Pick one workflow, connect it to a number your CFO cares about, and go deep — that's the restructuring pattern that's actually working.
BusinessWire / Forrester
3
54% of Enterprises Are Cutting Martech Vendors
  • A Redpoint Ventures report found 45% of CIOs are shifting budget to AI by cutting existing software — and 54% are actively consolidating vendors.
  • Traditional SaaS companies trade at 4.1x revenue while AI-native startups fetch 61x, which means M&A is about to accelerate.
  • If your martech vendor isn't building AI into the core product, they're probably an acquisition target — start scenario-planning for stack disruption.
Digital Commerce 360
4
Experimental Budgets Are Quietly Growing Past 20%
  • The old 80/20 core-to-experimental split is shifting toward 75/25 as AI creates ad surfaces that didn't exist a year ago — ChatGPT ads, AI Overviews, conversational placements.
  • The Knot is already running ChatGPT ad pilots; Pawco dedicates 15-20% of budget to AI-native channel tests.
  • If your test-and-learn budget is still a rounding error, you're ceding first-mover advantage on the channels that will set next year's benchmarks.
Digiday
5
White House Backs One Federal AI Standard
  • The new policy framework recommends federal preemption of state AI laws — one national standard instead of the current patchwork affecting targeting and personalization.
  • It also declares that training AI on copyrighted material doesn't violate copyright, which matters for every team using generative AI for content.
  • Compliance planning just got simpler if this holds — but the real move is accelerating AI content programs while the legal window is wide open.
