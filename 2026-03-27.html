AI in Marketing
5 Bullet Friday
March 27, 2026

The gap between AI marketing's promise and its results is closing — faster than most teams are ready for. Buyers are researching us through AI engines before we can track them, consumer trust sits 40 points below our own optimism, and the FTC just drew its first line in the AI vendor sand.

1
Adobe and NVIDIA: Agentic Creative Goes Infrastructure
  • Adobe and NVIDIA co-built next-gen Firefly models, agentic GenStudio campaign workflows, and marketing 3D digital twins — now in public beta — announced at GTC 2026.
  • Adobe's CEO simultaneously opened a succession process; the timing signals this partnership is the company's defining strategic move, not a product announcement.
  • If GenStudio is still a pilot in your org, this is the signal to move it to a budget line and a staffing plan — the creative infrastructure layer just became a commitment.
Adobe Newsroom
2
Buyers Are Shortlisting You in ChatGPT First
  • Forrester: 69% of B2B CMOs now name AI visibility — showing up in ChatGPT, Copilot, and Google AI Mode — as a top CEO priority.
  • Buyers are reaching a shortlist through AI engines before any tracked touchpoint; your funnel metrics have a blind spot at the very top.
  • GEO (generative engine optimization) needs its own budget line — if you're absent from the AI answer, you're not in the consideration set before the first MQL is logged.
Forrester
3
82% of Marketers Trust AI. 42% of Consumers Do.
  • AMA New York analyzed 18,804 social conversations: 82% of marketers expect AI to benefit consumers — only 42% of consumers agree, a 40-point trust gap.
  • 51% of marketers are already running campaigns targeting AI models and crawlers, not humans — a practice consumers are almost entirely unaware of.
  • In B2B, 44% of business buyers are ready for AI purchasing agents today vs. 19% of consumers — a structurally different trust curve that justifies accelerated investment in structured, machine-readable content now.
GlobeNewswire / AMA New York
4
The AI Content Graveyard Is a Real Problem
  • Three B2B failures are compounding: AI SDR vendors burning through customers (11x hit 70–80% churn at a $350M valuation), content output up 5–10x with flat performance, and 70% of teams unable to measure AI ROI.
  • AI removed the friction that used to force strategic prioritization — more assets get produced because they're cheap to make, not because there's a strategy behind them.
  • Before any AI content or SDR budget approval, require a specific measurement framework — no defined success metric means the failure mode is already built in.
Medium
5
The FTC Just Drew Its First AI Vendor Line
  • The FTC banned Air AI from marketing business opportunities and issued an $18M judgment for deceptive claims about AI-driven earnings and business growth — the first major action targeting an AI martech vendor's performance claims directly.
  • This isn't about the product's output — it's about what the vendor promised the product would do for your business, and that changes the procurement calculus.
  • Loop legal and compliance into AI tool vendor evaluations now; "AI-powered ROI" claims in contracts need verification and performance accountability clauses before you sign.
Federal Trade Commission
