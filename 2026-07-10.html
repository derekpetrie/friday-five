This week drew a line between teams using AI tools and teams that actually restructured around them.

1
HubSpot Buys Its Way Into Intent Data
  • Agreed to acquire Warmly, which identifies individual website visitors and deploys AI agents to engage them in real time.
  • Person-level intent signals and autonomous sales agents now live inside the CRM — no more stacking standalone tools.
  • If you're on HubSpot, your intent data vendor contracts just got a countdown clock.
Warmly Blog
2
60% Use AI Weekly, 10% Changed Anything
  • McKinsey's Cannes debrief found widespread AI tool adoption but almost no workflow restructuring for business impact.
  • The bottleneck isn't technology — teams layer AI onto old processes instead of rebuilding around it.
  • Stop asking "are we using AI?" and start asking "have we changed a single workflow because of it?"
McKinsey & Company
3
SEO Rankings Don't Mean AI Visibility
  • CiteLens found Google AI Mode pulls 90% of citations from top-10 results; ChatGPT pulls only 30% from those same pages.
  • Strong Google rankings can leave you completely invisible to ChatGPT's 900M weekly users.
  • You need a separate visibility strategy per AI engine — one SEO playbook won't cover it.
MarTech Series
4
Google Put an AI Sales Rep Inside Ads
  • Business Agent for Leads is a Gemini-powered agent that answers prospect questions inside the ad using your site content.
  • Instead of click-to-landing-page, the ad runs the discovery call and only surfaces a form after qualifying.
  • Testing in education, auto, and real estate now — prepare your website content for agent consumption.

5
AI Traffic Converts 42% Higher Than Average
  • Adobe found visitors from ChatGPT, Perplexity, and AI Mode convert 42% above average and spend 48% more time on-site.
  • AI-referred traffic grew 393% year-over-year — no longer a rounding error in your analytics.
  • You can't buy this traffic with ads — you earn it by being the source AI engines trust.
